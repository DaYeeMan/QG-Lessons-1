Introduction

Marginal distributions describe the probabilities of a subset of variables, ignoring the others. Conditional distributions describe the probability of one variable given the value of another. These concepts are essential for understanding dependencies and independence in probability.

Key Concepts & Formulas

  • Marginal PMF/PDF: \( P_X(x) = \sum_y P(X = x, Y = y) \) (discrete), \( f_X(x) = \int f_{X,Y}(x, y) dy \) (continuous)
  • Conditional PMF/PDF: \( P_{Y|X}(y|x) = \frac{P(X = x, Y = y)}{P_X(x)} \) (discrete), \( f_{Y|X}(y|x) = \frac{f_{X,Y}(x, y)}{f_X(x)} \) (continuous)

Worked Example

Suppose X and Y are the outcomes of rolling two fair dice. The marginal PMF for X is:

\[ P_X(x) = \sum_{y=1}^6 P(X = x, Y = y) = 6 \times (1/36) = 1/6 \]

If we know X = 3, the conditional PMF for Y is:

\[ P_{Y|X}(y|3) = \frac{P(X=3, Y=y)}{P_X(3)} = \frac{1/36}{1/6} = 1/6 \]
