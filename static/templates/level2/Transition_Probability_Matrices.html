Introduction

A transition probability matrix describes the probabilities of moving from one state to another in a Markov chain. Each row sums to 1, and the (i, j) entry gives the probability of moving from state i to state j in one step.

Key Concepts & Formulas

  • Definition: For a Markov chain with n states, the transition matrix P is an n√ón matrix where
    \[ P_{ij} = P(X_{n+1} = j | X_n = i) \]
  • Properties: All entries are between 0 and 1, and each row sums to 1.
  • n-step Transition: The probability of moving from i to j in n steps is the (i, j) entry of \( P^n \).

Worked Example

Consider the matrix

\[ P = \begin{pmatrix} 0.7 & 0.3 \\ 0.2 & 0.8 \end{pmatrix} \]

The probability of going from state 0 to state 1 in two steps is the (0,1) entry of \( P^2 \).
