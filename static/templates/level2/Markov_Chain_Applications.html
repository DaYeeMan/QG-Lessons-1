Introduction

Markov chains have a wide range of applications in finance, science, and engineering. They are used to model systems that evolve randomly over time with the Markov property.

Key Concepts & Examples

  • Credit Ratings: Modeling the probability of a company moving between credit ratings over time.
  • Weather Prediction: Modeling transitions between weather states (e.g., sunny, rainy).
  • Queueing Systems: Modeling customer arrivals and service in banks, call centers, etc.
  • Stock Price Regimes: Modeling bull and bear markets as states in a Markov chain.

Worked Example

Suppose a company can be in one of three credit ratings: A, B, or C. The transition matrix is:

\[ P = \begin{pmatrix} 0.9 & 0.08 & 0.02 \\ 0.1 & 0.85 & 0.05 \\ 0.05 & 0.15 & 0.8 \end{pmatrix} \]

This matrix can be used to compute the probability of a company moving from A to C in two years, etc.

Problem 3

What is the Markov property and why is it important for these applications?

The future depends only on the present state, not the past. This makes modeling and computation tractable.
