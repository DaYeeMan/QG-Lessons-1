Introduction

Probability transformations involve finding the distribution of a function of a random variable (or variables). This is essential for modeling derived quantities in finance and statistics.

Key Concepts & Formulas

  • Transformation of a Single Variable: If \( Y = g(X) \), the PDF of Y is
    \[ f_Y(y) = f_X(g^{-1}(y)) \left| \frac{d}{dy}g^{-1}(y) \right| \]
    (for monotonic g).
  • Linear Transformation: If \( Y = aX + b \), then \( E[Y] = aE[X] + b \), \( \operatorname{Var}(Y) = a^2 \operatorname{Var}(X) \).
  • Multivariate Transformation (Jacobian): For \( (U, V) = g(X, Y) \),
    \[ f_{U,V}(u, v) = f_{X,Y}(x(u,v), y(u,v)) \left| J \right| \]
    where \( J \) is the Jacobian determinant.

Worked Example

Let X be uniform on [0,1]. Find the PDF of \( Y = 2X \).

\[ f_Y(y) = f_X(y/2) \cdot \left| \frac{d}{dy}(y/2) \right| = 1 \cdot 1/2 = 1/2, \quad 0 < y < 2 \]

Problem 1

If X is exponential(1), what is the PDF of Y = 3X?

\( f_Y(y) = (1/3) e^{-y/3} \) for \( y > 0 \)



Problem 2

State the formula for the PDF of a function of two variables using the Jacobian.

Problem 3

Why is the Jacobian needed in multivariate transformations?

The Jacobian accounts for how areas/volumes are stretched or compressed by the transformation, ensuring the total probability remains 1.
