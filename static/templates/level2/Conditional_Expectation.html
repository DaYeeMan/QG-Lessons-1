Introduction

Conditional expectation is the expected value of a random variable given that another variable is known. It is a key concept in probability, statistics, and finance (e.g., pricing, risk management).

Key Concepts & Formulas

  • Discrete Case:
    \[ E[Y|X=x] = \sum_y y \cdot P(Y=y|X=x) \]
  • Continuous Case:
    \[ E[Y|X=x] = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} y f_{Y|X}(y|x) dy \]
  • Law of Iterated Expectations:
    \[ E[Y] = E[E[Y|X]] \]

Worked Example

Suppose X is the outcome of a fair die, and Y = 2X. What is E[Y|X]?

\[ E[Y|X] = 2X \]

The conditional expectation is a function of X.
