Introduction

The Tower Property (also called the law of iterated expectations) is a fundamental result in probability theory. It allows us to compute expectations in stages and is widely used in statistics, finance, and stochastic processes.

Key Concepts & Formulas

  • Statement:
    \[ E[E[Y|X]] = E[Y] \]
  • Generalization: For random variables X, Y, Z:
    \[ E[E[E[Y|X, Z]|X]] = E[Y] \]
  • Interpretation: The expected value of the conditional expectation is the unconditional expectation.

Worked Example

Suppose X is uniform on {1,2}, and Y|X=x is uniform on {x, x+1}. What is E[Y]?

\[ E[Y|X=1] = 1.5, \quad E[Y|X=2] = 2.5 \]
\[ E[Y] = E[E[Y|X]] = 0.5 \times 1.5 + 0.5 \times 2.5 = 2 \]

Practice Problems

Problem 1

State the tower property in your own words.

The expected value of the conditional expectation is the overall expected value.



Problem 2

Suppose X is uniform on {1,2,3}, and Y|X=x is uniform on {x, x+2}. What is E[Y]?

E[Y|X=x] = x+1, so E[Y] = (1+2+3)/3 + 1 = 3

Why is the tower property useful in finance?

It allows us to break down complex expectations (e.g., option pricing, risk) into simpler conditional steps.
