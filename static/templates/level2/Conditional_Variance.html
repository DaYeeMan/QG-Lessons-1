Introduction

Conditional variance measures the variability of a random variable given the value of another variable. It is a key concept in probability, statistics, and risk management.

Key Concepts & Formulas

  • Definition:
    \[ \operatorname{Var}(Y|X) = E[(Y - E[Y|X])^2|X] \]
  • Law of Total Variance:
    \[ \operatorname{Var}(Y) = E[\operatorname{Var}(Y|X)] + \operatorname{Var}(E[Y|X]) \]

Worked Example

Suppose X is uniform on {1,2}, and Y|X=x is uniform on {x, x+2}. What is \( \operatorname{Var}(Y) \)?

\[ \operatorname{Var}(Y|X=x) = \operatorname{Var}(\text{Uniform}(x, x+2)) = \frac{1}{3} \]
\[ E[Y|X=x] = x+1, \quad \operatorname{Var}(E[Y|X]) = \operatorname{Var}(x+1) = \frac{1}{3} \]
\[ \operatorname{Var}(Y) = E[\operatorname{Var}(Y|X)] + \operatorname{Var}(E[Y|X]) = \frac{1}{3} + \frac{1}{3} = \frac{2}{3} \]
