Introduction

The Chapman-Kolmogorov equations are fundamental equations in the theory of Markov chains that describe how transition probabilities evolve over time. They provide a way to compute multi-step transition probabilities from single-step transitions.

Key Concepts & Formulas

  • Chapman-Kolmogorov Equations: For any states i, j and times m, n:
    \[ P_{ij}^{(m+n)} = \sum_k P_{ik}^{(m)} P_{kj}^{(n)} \]
  • Matrix Form: In matrix notation:
    \[ P^{(m+n)} = P^{(m)} \cdot P^{(n)} \]
  • n-step Transition Probability: The probability of going from state i to state j in n steps.
  • Time Homogeneity: The equations assume the Markov chain is time-homogeneous (transition probabilities don't depend on the current time).

Worked Example

Consider a simple 2-state Markov chain with transition matrix:

\[ P = \begin{pmatrix} 0.7 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.6 \end{pmatrix} \]

To find the 2-step transition probability from state 1 to state 2:

\[ P_{12}^{(2)} = P_{11} \cdot P_{12} + P_{12} \cdot P_{22} = 0.7 \cdot 0.3 + 0.3 \cdot 0.6 = 0.39 \]

Problem 2

What is the interpretation of the Chapman-Kolmogorov equations in terms of path decomposition?

The equations decompose the path from i to j in m+n steps into all possible intermediate states k at time m. The probability is the sum over all such intermediate states.



Problem 3

How do the Chapman-Kolmogorov equations relate to matrix multiplication?

The equations show that n-step transition matrices are obtained by raising the one-step transition matrix to the nth power: \( P^{(n)} = P^n \).
