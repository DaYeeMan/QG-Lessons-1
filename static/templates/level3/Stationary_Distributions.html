Introduction

Stationary distributions describe the long-term behavior of Markov chains. If a chain is started in its stationary distribution, it remains in that distribution at all future times.

Key Concepts & Formulas

  • Stationary Distribution: A probability vector \( \pi \) such that:
    \[ \pi P = \pi \]
  • Existence & Uniqueness: For irreducible, aperiodic, positive recurrent chains, a unique stationary distribution exists.
  • Convergence: For such chains, the distribution of the chain converges to \( \pi \) regardless of the initial state.
  • Detailed Balance: If \( \pi_i P_{ij} = \pi_j P_{ji} \) for all i, j, then \( \pi \) is a stationary distribution (reversible chain).
  • Computation: Solve the system \( \pi P = \pi \), \( \sum_i \pi_i = 1 \).

Worked Example

Consider a Markov chain with transition matrix:

\[ P = \begin{pmatrix} 0.7 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.6 \end{pmatrix} \]

To find the stationary distribution \( \pi \), solve:

\[ \pi_1 = 0.7\pi_1 + 0.4\pi_2 \\ \pi_2 = 0.3\pi_1 + 0.6\pi_2 \\ \pi_1 + \pi_2 = 1 \]

The solution is \( \pi = (0.57, 0.43) \).

Practice Problems

Problem 1

For the above chain, verify that \( \pi \) is a stationary distribution.

Compute \( \pi P \) and check that it equals \( \pi \). \( 0.57*0.7 + 0.43*0.4 = 0.57 \), \( 0.57*0.3 + 0.43*0.6 = 0.43 \).



Problem 2

Explain the significance of detailed balance in Markov chains.

Detailed balance ensures reversibility, which simplifies analysis and is important in applications like Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC).



Problem 3

How do you find the stationary distribution for a Markov chain with more than two states?

Set up the system \( \pi P = \pi \) and \( \sum_i \pi_i = 1 \), then solve the resulting linear equations, often using matrix methods or Gaussian elimination.
