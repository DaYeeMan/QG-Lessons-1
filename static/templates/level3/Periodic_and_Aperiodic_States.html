Introduction

Periodic and aperiodic states are classifications in Markov chains that describe the regularity with which a state can be revisited. This property is crucial for understanding convergence to equilibrium and the existence of limiting distributions.

Key Concepts & Formulas

  • Period of a State: The period of state i is defined as:
    \[ d(i) = \gcd\{ n \geq 1 : P_{ii}^{(n)} > 0 \} \]
  • Aperiodic State: State i is aperiodic if d(i) = 1. Otherwise, it is periodic with period d(i) > 1.
  • Class Property: All states in a communicating class share the same period.
  • Implication for Convergence: Aperiodicity is required for the Markov chain to converge to a stationary distribution in the usual sense.
  • Cesàro Mean: For periodic chains, the Cesàro mean of transition probabilities may converge even if the usual limit does not.

Worked Example

Consider a Markov chain with transition matrix:

\[ P = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \]

Both states have period 2 (can only return in even steps). The chain is periodic and does not converge to a stationary distribution in the usual sense.

Now consider a chain with self-loops:

\[ P = \begin{pmatrix} 0.5 & 0.5 \\ 0.5 & 0.5 \end{pmatrix} \]

Both states are aperiodic (period 1), and the chain converges to the stationary distribution.

Practice Problems

Problem 1

Show that in an irreducible Markov chain, all states in a communicating class have the same period.

If i communicates with j, then for any n such that P_{ij}^{(n)} > 0 and m such that P_{ji}^{(m)} > 0, the set of return times to i is closed under addition with n + m. Thus, periods must match across the class.



Problem 2

For the chain with P = [[0,1],[1,0]], compute the Cesàro mean of the transition probabilities.

The Cesàro mean is the average of the transition matrices over n steps. For this chain, the Cesàro mean converges to [[0.5,0.5],[0.5,0.5]].



Problem 3

Why is aperiodicity necessary for convergence to equilibrium?

In periodic chains, the probability of being in a state oscillates and does not settle to a fixed value. Aperiodicity ensures the chain "mixes" and converges to the stationary distribution.
