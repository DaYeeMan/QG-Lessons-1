Introduction

Queueing theory is the mathematical study of waiting lines and systems where customers arrive, wait for service, and depart. It is fundamental in operations research, telecommunications, and service system design.

Key Concepts & Formulas

  • M/M/1 Queue: A queue with Poisson arrivals, exponential service times, and one server. The steady-state probability of n customers in the system is:
    \[ P_n = (1 - \rho) \rho^n \]
    where ρ = λ/μ is the traffic intensity.
  • Little's Law: L = λW, where L is the average number of customers in the system, λ is the arrival rate, and W is the average waiting time.
  • Utilization Factor: ρ = λ/μ, which must be less than 1 for stability.
  • Average Queue Length: L_q = ρ²/(1-ρ) for M/M/1.
  • Average Waiting Time: W_q = L_q/λ = ρ/(μ(1-ρ)) for M/M/1.
  • Erlang B Formula: Used for loss systems where customers are blocked if all servers are busy.

Worked Example

Consider an M/M/1 queue with arrival rate λ = 4 customers per hour and service rate μ = 6 customers per hour. The traffic intensity is:

\[ \rho = \frac{\lambda}{\mu} = \frac{4}{6} = \frac{2}{3} \]

The average number of customers in the system is:

\[ L = \frac{\rho}{1-\rho} = \frac{2/3}{1-2/3} = 2 \]

And the average waiting time is W = L/λ = 2/4 = 0.5 hours = 30 minutes.

Practice Problems

Problem 1

For an M/M/1 queue with λ = 3 and μ = 5, find the probability that there are exactly 2 customers in the system.

ρ = 3/5 = 0.6. P₂ = (1-0.6)(0.6)² = 0.4 × 0.36 = 0.144.



Problem 2

Explain the significance of Little's Law in queueing theory.

Little's Law provides a fundamental relationship between arrival rate, system size, and waiting time that holds for any stable queueing system, regardless of the arrival or service distributions.



Problem 3

How do you extend the M/M/1 analysis to M/M/c queues with multiple servers?

For M/M/c, the traffic intensity becomes ρ = λ/(cμ), and the steady-state probabilities and performance measures are computed using more complex formulas involving the Erlang C formula.
