Introduction

Hidden Markov Models are widely used in signal processing for modeling time-varying signals, noise reduction, and pattern recognition. In financial applications, they can be used for filtering market noise, detecting structural breaks, and extracting meaningful patterns from high-frequency data.

Key Concepts & Formulas

  • Signal Model: Observable signal with hidden state-dependent parameters:
    \[ y_t = \mu_{s_t} + \sigma_{s_t} \epsilon_t \]
    where \(\epsilon_t \sim N(0,1)\) and \(s_t\) is the hidden state.
  • Kalman Filter Integration: Combining HMM with linear state-space model:
    \[ x_t = F_{s_t} x_{t-1} + w_t, \quad y_t = H_{s_t} x_t + v_t \]
  • Signal Filtering: Optimal estimate of hidden signal:
    \[ \hat{x}_t = \sum_{i=1}^N \gamma_t(i) \hat{x}_t^{(i)} \]
    where \(\hat{x}_t^{(i)}\) is the estimate conditional on regime i.
  • Change Point Detection: Probability of regime change:
    \[ P(s_t \neq s_{t-1}|y_1,...,y_t) = \sum_{i \neq j} \xi_{t-1}(i,j) \]
  • Signal-to-Noise Ratio: Regime-dependent SNR:
    \[ \text{SNR}_i = \frac{\mu_i^2}{\sigma_i^2} \]

Worked Example

Consider filtering a noisy price signal with two regimes (trending and mean-reverting):

\[ \begin{align} \mu_1 &= 0.001, \quad \sigma_1 = 0.01 \quad \text{(Trending)} \\ \mu_2 &= 0, \quad \sigma_2 = 0.02 \quad \text{(Mean-reverting)} \end{align} \]

Transition matrix:

\[ A = \begin{pmatrix} 0.9 & 0.1 \\ 0.2 & 0.8 \end{pmatrix} \]

For observed signal \(y_t = 0.005\) and regime probabilities \(\gamma_t = [0.7, 0.3]\):

\[ \begin{align} \hat{x}_t^{(1)} &= 0.001 + \frac{0.01^2}{0.01^2 + 0.01^2}(0.005 - 0.001) = 0.003 \\ \hat{x}_t^{(2)} &= 0 + \frac{0.02^2}{0.02^2 + 0.01^2}(0.005 - 0) = 0.004 \\ \hat{x}_t &= 0.7 \cdot 0.003 + 0.3 \cdot 0.004 = 0.0033 \end{align} \]

Practice Problems

Problem 1

How do HMMs improve signal filtering compared to traditional methods?

HMMs can adapt to changing signal characteristics by switching between different filtering regimes. They provide regime-dependent optimal estimates and can handle non-stationary signals better than fixed-parameter filters.



Problem 2

What is the advantage of combining HMMs with Kalman filters?

The combination allows for regime-dependent state dynamics while maintaining the optimal filtering properties of Kalman filters. It can handle both discrete regime changes and continuous state evolution simultaneously.



Problem 3

How can HMMs be used for detecting structural breaks in financial time series?

HMMs can identify regime changes by monitoring the transition probabilities and regime probabilities. Sudden changes in these probabilities indicate potential structural breaks in the underlying data generating process.
