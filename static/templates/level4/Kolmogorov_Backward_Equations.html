Introduction

The Kolmogorov Backward Equations are an alternative system of differential equations that also describe the evolution of transition probabilities in continuous-time Markov chains. While the forward equations focus on the final state, the backward equations focus on the initial state and are often more useful for certain types of problems.

Key Concepts & Formulas

  • Backward Equations: For all states i, j and times t â‰¥ 0:
    \[ \frac{d}{dt}P_{ij}(t) = \sum_k q_{ik}P_{kj}(t) \]
  • Matrix Form: In matrix notation:
    \[ \frac{d}{dt}P(t) = QP(t) \]
  • Initial Condition: \(P(0) = I\) (identity matrix)
  • Solution: The solution is the same matrix exponential:
    \[ P(t) = e^{Qt} \]
  • Interpretation: The backward equations consider the first transition from the initial state and then use the Markov property.
  • Equivalence: Both forward and backward equations give the same solution \(P(t) = e^{Qt}\).

Worked Example

For the same two-state CTMC with transition rate matrix:

\[ Q = \begin{pmatrix} -2 & 2 \\ 1 & -1 \end{pmatrix} \]

The Kolmogorov backward equations are:

\[ \begin{align} \frac{d}{dt}P_{00}(t) &= (-2)P_{00}(t) + 2P_{10}(t) \\ \frac{d}{dt}P_{01}(t) &= (-2)P_{01}(t) + 2P_{11}(t) \\ \frac{d}{dt}P_{10}(t) &= 1 \cdot P_{00}(t) + (-1)P_{10}(t) \\ \frac{d}{dt}P_{11}(t) &= 1 \cdot P_{01}(t) + (-1)P_{11}(t) \end{align} \]

Practice Problems

Problem 1

What is the key difference between forward and backward equations?

Forward equations: \(\frac{d}{dt}P_{ij}(t) = \sum_k P_{ik}(t)q_{kj}\) (focus on final state j)
Backward equations: \(\frac{d}{dt}P_{ij}(t) = \sum_k q_{ik}P_{kj}(t)\) (focus on initial state i)

Write the backward equation for \(P_{12}(t)\) in a three-state CTMC.

Problem 3

When might you prefer to use backward equations over forward equations?

Backward equations are often preferred when you know the final state and want to work backwards, or when analyzing hitting times and absorption probabilities.
