Introduction

The Kolmogorov Forward Equations are a system of differential equations that describe how the transition probabilities of a continuous-time Markov chain evolve over time. They provide a fundamental way to compute \(P_{ij}(t)\) - the probability of being in state j at time t given that the process started in state i at time 0.

Key Concepts & Formulas

  • Forward Equations: For all states i, j and times t â‰¥ 0:
    \[ \frac{d}{dt}P_{ij}(t) = \sum_k P_{ik}(t)q_{kj} \]
  • Matrix Form: In matrix notation:
    \[ \frac{d}{dt}P(t) = P(t)Q \]
  • Initial Condition: \(P(0) = I\) (identity matrix)
  • Solution: The solution is given by the matrix exponential:
    \[ P(t) = e^{Qt} \]
  • Interpretation: The forward equations describe the rate of change of transition probabilities by considering all possible paths from state i to state j.

Worked Example

Consider a two-state CTMC with transition rate matrix:

\[ Q = \begin{pmatrix} -2 & 2 \\ 1 & -1 \end{pmatrix} \]

The Kolmogorov forward equations are:

\[ \begin{align} \frac{d}{dt}P_{00}(t) &= P_{00}(t)(-2) + P_{01}(t)(1) \\ \frac{d}{dt}P_{01}(t) &= P_{00}(t)(2) + P_{01}(t)(-1) \\ \frac{d}{dt}P_{10}(t) &= P_{10}(t)(-2) + P_{11}(t)(1) \\ \frac{d}{dt}P_{11}(t) &= P_{10}(t)(2) + P_{11}(t)(-1) \end{align} \]

Practice Problems

Problem 1

What is the physical interpretation of the Kolmogorov forward equations?

The forward equations describe how the probability of being in state j at time t changes over time, considering all possible paths that could lead to state j from any other state k.

Problem 3

What is the relationship between the forward equations and the matrix exponential solution?

The matrix exponential \(P(t) = e^{Qt}\) is the formal solution to the forward equations \(\frac{d}{dt}P(t) = P(t)Q\) with initial condition \(P(0) = I\).
