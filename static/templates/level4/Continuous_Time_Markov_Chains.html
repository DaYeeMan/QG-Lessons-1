Introduction

Continuous-Time Markov Chains (CTMCs) are stochastic processes that evolve continuously in time, where the future state depends only on the current state (Markov property). Unlike discrete-time Markov chains, CTMCs can change states at any time instant, not just at discrete time steps.

Key Concepts & Formulas

  • Markov Property: For any s, t ≥ 0 and states i, j, k:
    \[ P(X(t+s) = j | X(s) = i, X(u) = k \text{ for } u < s) = P(X(t+s) = j | X(s) = i) \]
  • Transition Rate Matrix Q: The infinitesimal generator matrix where:
    \[ q_{ij} = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{P(X(h) = j | X(0) = i)}{h}, \quad i \neq j \]
    \[ q_{ii} = -\sum_{j \neq i} q_{ij} \]
  • Sojourn Time: Time spent in state i before transitioning is exponentially distributed with rate \(-q_{ii}\).
  • Transition Probabilities: Given by the matrix exponential:
    \[ P(t) = e^{Qt} \]

Worked Example

Consider a CTMC with two states (0 and 1) and transition rate matrix:

\[ Q = \begin{pmatrix} -2 & 2 \\ 1 & -1 \end{pmatrix} \]

This means:

  • From state 0: transition to state 1 at rate 2
  • From state 1: transition to state 0 at rate 1
  • Sojourn time in state 0: exponential with mean 1/2
  • Sojourn time in state 1: exponential with mean 1

Practice Problems

Problem 1

What is the difference between discrete-time and continuous-time Markov chains?

Discrete-time Markov chains change states only at discrete time steps, while continuous-time Markov chains can change states at any time instant. CTMCs use transition rates instead of transition probabilities and have exponentially distributed sojourn times.



Problem 2

For a CTMC with transition rate matrix Q, what is the probability of staying in state i for time t?

\( P(\text{stay in state } i \text{ for time } t) = e^{q_{ii}t} = e^{-|q_{ii}|t} \)

Write the transition rate matrix for a three-state CTMC where transitions occur at rates: 0→1: 2, 1→2: 3, 2→0: 1, 1→0: 1, 2→1: 2.

\( Q = \begin{pmatrix} -2 & 2 & 0 \\ 1 & -4 & 3 \\ 1 & 2 & -3 \end{pmatrix} \)
