Introduction

Continuous-Time Markov Chains have numerous applications in quantitative finance, including credit risk modeling, interest rate modeling, regime-switching models, and option pricing. They provide a flexible framework for modeling stochastic processes that can change states continuously over time.

Key Concepts & Formulas

  • Credit Rating Transitions: Modeling how credit ratings change over time:
    \[ Q = \begin{pmatrix} q_{AA} & q_{AB} & q_{AC} & \cdots \\ q_{BA} & q_{BB} & q_{BC} & \cdots \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots \end{pmatrix} \]
  • Regime-Switching Models: Asset returns follow different distributions in different market regimes:
    \[ dS_t = \mu(X_t)S_t dt + \sigma(X_t)S_t dW_t \]
    where \(X_t\) is a CTMC representing the market regime.
  • Interest Rate Models: Short rate follows a CTMC:
    \[ r_t = r(X_t) \]
    where \(r(i)\) is the interest rate in state i.
  • Default Intensity: The default intensity in state i is \(-q_{ii}\).
  • Survival Probability: Probability of no default by time t:
    \[ P(\tau > t) = \sum_i \pi_i(0) \sum_j P_{ij}(t) \]

Worked Example

Consider a credit rating model with three states: Investment Grade (IG), Speculative Grade (SG), and Default (D). The transition rate matrix is:

\[ Q = \begin{pmatrix} -0.1 & 0.08 & 0.02 \\ 0.05 & -0.15 & 0.10 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \]

This means:

  • IG companies transition to SG at rate 0.08, default at rate 0.02
  • SG companies improve to IG at rate 0.05, default at rate 0.10
  • Default is an absorbing state (no transitions out)

The survival probability for an IG company over time t is:

\[ P(\text{survive}) = P_{IG,IG}(t) + P_{IG,SG}(t) \]

Practice Problems

Problem 1

What is the advantage of using CTMCs for credit risk modeling?

CTMCs allow for continuous monitoring of credit quality changes, capture the time-varying nature of default risk, and provide a natural framework for modeling rating transitions and default events.



Problem 2

In a regime-switching model, how does the volatility change when the market regime changes?

The volatility \(\sigma(X_t)\) is a function of the current regime state. When the regime changes, the volatility immediately jumps to the new level associated with that regime.



Problem 3

What is the relationship between the transition rate matrix and default intensity?

The default intensity in state i is \(-q_{ii}\), which represents the rate at which the process leaves state i (including transitions to default). The total exit rate from state i is \(-q_{ii}\).
