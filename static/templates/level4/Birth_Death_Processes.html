Introduction

A Birth-Death Process is a special type of continuous-time Markov chain where transitions can only occur between adjacent states. The process can only increase by one (birth) or decrease by one (death) at each transition. These processes are fundamental in queueing theory, population dynamics, and many other applications.

Key Concepts & Formulas

  • Definition: A CTMC \(\{X(t), t \geq 0\}\) with state space \(\{0, 1, 2, ...\}\) is a birth-death process if:
    \[ q_{ij} = 0 \text{ for } |i-j| > 1 \]
  • Transition Rates:
    • \(\lambda_i = q_{i,i+1}\): birth rate in state i
    • \(\mu_i = q_{i,i-1}\): death rate in state i
    • \(q_{ii} = -(\lambda_i + \mu_i)\)
  • Transition Rate Matrix:
    \[ Q = \begin{pmatrix} -\lambda_0 & \lambda_0 & 0 & 0 & \cdots \\ \mu_1 & -(\lambda_1 + \mu_1) & \lambda_1 & 0 & \cdots \\ 0 & \mu_2 & -(\lambda_2 + \mu_2) & \lambda_2 & \cdots \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots \end{pmatrix} \]
  • Stationary Distribution: If it exists:
    \[ \pi_i = \pi_0 \prod_{k=1}^i \frac{\lambda_{k-1}}{\mu_k} \]

Worked Example

Consider a simple birth-death process with constant rates \(\lambda = 2\) and \(\mu = 1\):

\[ Q = \begin{pmatrix} -2 & 2 & 0 & 0 & \cdots \\ 1 & -3 & 2 & 0 & \cdots \\ 0 & 1 & -3 & 2 & \cdots \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots \end{pmatrix} \]

The stationary distribution is:

\[ \pi_i = \pi_0 \left(\frac{2}{1}\right)^i = \pi_0 \cdot 2^i \]

Since \(\sum_{i=0}^{\infty} \pi_i = 1\), we have \(\pi_0 = \frac{1}{1 + 2 + 4 + 8 + \cdots} = 0\) (the process is transient).

Practice Problems

Problem 1

What is the key restriction on transitions in a birth-death process?

Transitions can only occur between adjacent states - the process can only increase or decrease by one unit at each transition.



Problem 2

For a birth-death process with \(\lambda_i = 3\) and \(\mu_i = 2\) for all i, what is the stationary distribution?

\(\pi_i = \pi_0 \left(\frac{3}{2}\right)^i\). Since \(\frac{3}{2} > 1\), the series diverges and \(\pi_0 = 0\), so the process is transient.



Problem 3

Give an example of a real-world system that can be modeled as a birth-death process.

Queue length in a service system, population size in ecology, number of customers in a store, or inventory levels in a warehouse.
